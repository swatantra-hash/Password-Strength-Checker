Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2, 2026

PassGuard ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how information is handled when you use our password strength analysis tool ("Service").

1. Information We Collect

We do not collect any personal information. PassGuard operates entirely within your web browser. Specifically:

  • Your passwords are never stored, transmitted to our servers, or logged anywhere.
  • We do not use cookies, tracking pixels, or any form of analytics.
  • We do not collect your IP address, browser fingerprint, or device information.
  • No user accounts, registration, or login is required.

2. Password Analysis

All password analysis — including strength scoring, entropy calculation, pattern detection, and character composition — is performed entirely client-side using JavaScript running in your browser. Your password never leaves your device during analysis.

3. Data Breach Check

When you use the "Check for Breaches" feature, PassGuard uses the Have I Been Pwned API with a technique called k-anonymity:

  • Your password is hashed using the SHA-1 algorithm entirely within your browser.
  • Only the first 5 characters of the hash (out of 40) are sent to the HIBP API.
  • The API returns a list of matching hash suffixes, which are compared locally in your browser.
  • Your full password and its complete hash are never transmitted over the network.

This means that even the Have I Been Pwned service cannot determine which password you are checking. For more information on this approach, see the HIBP API documentation.

4. Local Storage

PassGuard uses your browser's localStorage only to remember your theme preference (dark or light mode). This data:

  • Is stored only on your device
  • Is never transmitted to any server
  • Can be cleared at any time through your browser settings
  • Contains no personal or sensitive information

5. Third-Party Services

PassGuard uses the following third-party services:

  • Google Fonts — for loading web fonts (Inter, JetBrains Mono). Google may collect limited access logs as described in their privacy FAQ.
  • Have I Been Pwned API — for breach checking, using the k-anonymity model described above. See their privacy policy.

6. Children's Privacy

PassGuard does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. Since we do not collect any data whatsoever, this is inherently compliant with COPPA and similar regulations.
